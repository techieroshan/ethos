# Ethos User Personas (Platform-Wide, Open Network Model)

---

## Admin Persona

### Persona Name

Global Admin Alex

### Demographics

### Goals

* Ensure a secure, seamless, and highly available experience for all users across the global, open platform.

* Maintain platform-wide uptime and rapid response to incidents, supporting every user irrespective of organization.

* Manage user access and permissions at scale, ensuring consistency and reliability for all authenticated participants.

* Oversee compliance and audit readiness for the entire, publicly accessible network.

* Streamline onboarding and offboarding of users and administrators, focusing on universal access—not gating by organization.

* Facilitate integrations and configure platform-wide settings that affect all users, regardless of their configuration origin.

### Pain Points

* Managing global-scale permissions and security amidst an open, fully networked user base.

* Navigating multifaceted international compliance as it applies to a single, universally accessible system.

* Maintaining visibility and control across all public user activity and content, without organizational silos.

* Preventing and remediating configuration errors that could impact the platform's universal accessibility.

* Coordinating incident responses that affect all authenticated users, across time zones and contexts.

### User Journey

1. Reviews system-wide dashboards and health metrics for all users upon login.

2. Manages platform roles and permissions, ensuring global consistency and inclusivity.

3. Configures integrations, APIs, and security policies universally—they apply everywhere on the platform.

4. Reviews public audit trails and logs, accessible for all authenticated users.

5. Responds to security alerts or data privacy requests affecting any and all users.

6. Deploys configuration changes that impact the whole platform community, monitoring for network-wide effects.

7. Generates reports reflecting the platform’s holistic health, compliance, and participation network.

### Behavioral Traits

* Security- and privacy-conscious; vigilant for threats and risks that impact the entire global platform.

* Methodical and detail-oriented, balancing open participation with comprehensive oversight.

* Technically proficient, values transparency and ease of global collaboration.

* Strong communicator, especially during high-profile incidents affecting the platform at large.

* Proactive about network-wide risk assessment and mitigation in an open community.

### Quotes from User Research

* "Every change I make is immediately visible to thousands of users—so global impact and open feedback are always top-of-mind."

* "My job is to keep the platform safe and accessible for everyone, not just a siloed group."

* "Rapid feedback is critical. If there’s any platform-wide problem, I need to know—because it affects everyone, everywhere."

### Scenarios

* **Platform-Wide Outage Response:** Alex coordinates transparent communication and rapid recovery, restoring access and reassuring all authenticated users—no matter where they are or who they represent.

* **Global Audit Preparation:** Alex pulls and submits comprehensive, platform-wide audit logs for external and public review, representing the entire user community.

* **Universal Security Update:** Rolls out a new multi-factor authentication policy that applies to every single account and action on the platform, not restricted by organizational affiliation.

* **Delegated Admin Controls:** Grants admin rights to trusted users for platform configuration—not for gating content or participation—while tracking changes in publicly accessible logs.

## Organization Admin Persona

### Persona Name

Config Admin Olivia

### Demographics

### Goals

* Efficiently configure organization-specific settings and manage user roles for tailored workflows, aware that such configuration does not gate platform participation.

* Ensure the organization's users are compliant with both internal (company) standards and the platform’s global policies for all users.

* Support seamless onboarding/offboarding internally, while understanding that all users can engage fully with the platform’s public features.

* Personalize notifications and app integrations, without impacting the global, open participation model.

* Resolve permission and access questions at the configuration level, but recognize that content feedback/review is platform-wide and never organizationally restricted.

### Pain Points

* Clarifying the distinction between org-level configuration and platform-wide, open user participation.

* Responding quickly to internal support requests, ensuring that users understand the open, universal access model.

* Managing team compliance within their configuration space while keeping in mind global visibility and network effects.

* Adapting to updates in the platform that affect all users, including those outside of their configuration purview.

### User Journey

1. Configures notification, branding, and integration settings for their users—affecting experience, not access.

2. Responds to team requests for changes in workflow, permissions, or preferences—never gating public features or feedback mechanisms.

3. Audits configuration changes and usage patterns, recognizing that all content and feedback is universally viewable by authenticated users.

4. Collaborates with global admins to escalate technical or security incidents that may impact the entire platform.

5. Educates internal users on the difference between configuration and open participation.

### Behavioral Traits

* Pragmatic and solution-oriented, focused on making the platform work smoothly for their teams in a universally accessible environment.

* Seeks clarity and operational autonomy, but understands their role is configuration—not participation control.

* Communicates the open, public model to others, bridging awareness gaps for less tech-savvy colleagues.

### Quotes from User Research

* "I help our users set up for success, but I know everyone can engage globally—feedback and conversations aren’t stuck in our org."

* "We shape the workflow for our team, but the community is the network—all authenticated users, everywhere."

* "My users sometimes ask why certain things are visible to the whole platform—that’s just how open participation works here."

### Scenarios

* **Team Onboarding:** Olivia customizes the onboarding checklist and notifications for her team, knowing that once they're on the platform, they have the same global access as every user.

* **Configuration Adjustment:** Updates branding or integrations without affecting user access, participation, or feedback/review, which is never org-gated.

* **Internal Support:** Guides a colleague on how universality and open feedback works, ensuring they understand the public nature of reporting and review.

* **Security Preparation:** Coordinates with global admins during a network-wide security rollout, ensuring her organization's configuration is up to date, but knowing participation remains open and public.

## Moderator Persona

### Persona Name

Community Moderator Casey

### Demographics

### Goals

* Maintain a healthy, inclusive, and respectful environment for all users on the open, globally accessible platform.

* Enforce community guidelines platform-wide, with every authenticated user able to see, review, and participate across all areas, regardless of any configuration context.

* Efficiently process reports and flagged content knowing all reviews and moderation actions are public and never organization-restricted.

* Foster open communication, resolve conflicts, and model transparency in all actions.

### Pain Points

* High, platform-wide volume of flagged content—requires efficient, scalable moderation tools.

* Emotional toll of public, sometimes contentious, global interactions.

* Ambiguity handling for edge cases in a community where guidelines must be interpreted for all network participants worldwide.

* Expectations of transparency—every user can observe moderation outcomes and participate in feedback, making mistakes more visible.

### User Journey

1. Logs in and reviews a global queue of reported items, submitted by any user across the platform.

2. Evaluates content with a platform-wide perspective; moderation decisions are visible to all authenticated users.

3. Applies actions—warnings, content removals, user suspensions—that take effect globally and are public.

4. Documents rationales and flags complex scenarios for higher-level review, all actions remaining fully network-wide, not org-gated.

5. Engages with users who appeal or seek clarification, reinforcing open platform policies.

6. Participates in moderator training—best practices are shared across the global moderator network, not limited by organization.

### Behavioral Traits

* Empathetic and diplomatic, treating all users and content equally, regardless of original configuration or background.

* Prioritizes fairness, transparency, and the open participation model.

* Resilient, adapts to the public nature of global moderation.

### Quotes from User Research

* "Everyone sees the same things—I moderate for the entire platform, not for a single group."

* "Decisions here are open and visible to all. It keeps us accountable and the community healthy."

* "The volume can be daunting, but knowing my work impacts the whole network keeps me focused."

### Scenarios

* **Public Moderation:** Casey reviews content reported by users from across the entire platform, ensuring all actions are public and never org-restricted.

* **Transparency Dialogue:** Responds to user questions about moderation decisions, emphasizing universal policies.

* **Policy Updates:** Trains on global guideline changes, knowing these will apply everywhere, and every user is subject to the same visibility and standards.

* **Community Cleanup:** Executes a coordinated effort to address widespread network abuse, actions seen by all users platform-wide.

## Standard User Persona

### Persona Name

Everyday Emily

### Demographics

### Goals

* Seamlessly access and participate in the platform’s open network with all features and interactions public to authenticated users.

* Retain control over personal data and preferences while understanding interactions—content, reviews, and feedback—are visible platform-wide.

* Receive help and support openly, knowing resources and knowledge are available to all users, not siloed by organization.

* Collaborate and communicate freely with any other authenticated user, leveraging the universal, non-gated nature of the platform.

* Trust the security and accessibility of all global features.

### Pain Points

* Initial confusion about the open, public nature of feedback and review.

* Worry over the visibility of their actions and data in a platform where everyone participates globally.

* Difficulty finding universal (not org-scoped) help or support when needed.

* Occasional overload from open, network-wide notifications and interactions.

### User Journey

1. Receives platform invite, completes a universal onboarding flow designed for everyone.

2. Sets up profile and privacy controls, with clear messaging about global participation.

3. Uses core features—collaboration, content posting, feedback—knowing every action is visible and can be interacted with by any authenticated user.

4. Seeks help or contributes feedback through platform-wide, public mechanisms.

5. Adjusts settings for notifications and privacy, understanding these affect exposure across the entire network.

6. Signs out securely, confident preferences and visibility controls persist network-wide.

### Behavioral Traits

* Pragmatic and privacy-aware, values simplicity and transparency.

* Eager to explore and contribute, once comfortable with the public network model.

* Appreciates efficient support and platform-wide clarity.

### Quotes from User Research

* "When I comment or post, everyone can see it—it’s truly an open platform."

* "Support and feedback aren’t restricted to a group; it all happens in the open, and that’s both exciting and a little daunting."

* "The network feels connected. I can collaborate with anyone on the platform, right away."

### Scenarios

* **Global Feature Use:** Emily joins a discussion or gives feedback on a platform feature, open to and visible by every authenticated user.

* **Public Support:** Requests assistance via the publicly available helpdesk, gets solutions viewable and reusable by all.

* **Profile Update:** Edits her visibility settings, controlling how her information appears globally—not just within one group.

* **Feedback Participation:** Participates in a global user feedback survey, knowing responses are used to improve the entire open platform.

## Multi-Tenant User Persona

### Persona Name

Multi-Facet Morgan

### Demographics

### Goals

* Easily navigate multiple branding or workflow configurations without impacting participation, which remains global and open to all users.

* Keep work, data, and communications organized—even as participation and content review are never restricted by “tenant” boundaries.

* Understand that posting, reviewing, and providing feedback are always public—regardless of customization or configuration.

* Adapt preferences for personal workflow, knowing these are for efficiency, not for access or feedback gating.

### Pain Points

* Occasional confusion about the separation between appearance/configuration and the platform’s open participation logic.

* Wanting fine-grained notification and preference controls to manage global participation without information overload.

* Stress about maintaining personal organization in an environment where collaboration is always universally enabled.

### User Journey

1. Logs in, accesses different configuration profiles or contexts for work purposes—public platform network remains constant.

2. Engages in content posting, feedback, and reviews that are immediately visible to all authenticated users.

3. Manages notifications and preferences to best balance cross-context interactions without limiting open collaboration.

4. Reviews activity streams and ensures organization of files or tasks, with content always globally accessible.

5. Logs out or switches context for workflow efficiency, never for participation restriction.

### Behavioral Traits

* Organized, values personal efficiency in an openly accessible participation landscape.

* Embraces open, universal collaboration and content review.

* Adapts quickly between different contextual configurations, but anchors to the platform’s global networked environment.

### Quotes from User Research

* "Configuration helps me keep work streams tidy, but when I share or review, it’s broadcast to the whole platform."

* "Notifications and collaboration are always global here. You can’t just hide in one group, and that’s useful for real transparency."

* "Switching context helps me stay organized, not locked down. The network is open to all of us."

### Scenarios

* **Multi-Context Collaboration:** Morgan contributes to projects with different configurations, but all content and participation are network-wide.

* **Global Feedback:** Posts feedback visible to all users, no matter which workflow or branding context he’s working in.

* **Notification Optimization:** Tweaks notification preferences to handle global network visibility, not to limit participation scope.

* **Cross-Context Support:** Seeks platform-wide help, accessing resources shared with all authenticated users.

## Guest Persona

### Persona Name

Visitor Val

### Demographics

### Goals

* Seamlessly access public or shared platform resources, understanding all content and feedback/review are network-wide and never restricted.

* Complete discovery or transaction tasks with frictionless, open guest flows.

* Understand differences between guest and authenticated user participation, with open upgrade paths clearly explained.

* Feel confident about privacy and transparent about what information is public and network-visible.

### Pain Points

* Frustration with unexpected friction in an otherwise open, public experience.

* Uncertainty about what features are available without upgrading, given the broad visibility of content.

* Overexposure concerns: making sure guest activities are not inappropriately logged or surfaced.

* Lack of transparency about the full value of open, public participation without registration.

### User Journey

1. Accesses a public resource—document, forum, or project—immediately visible to all, as part of the open platform.

2. Leverages available guest actions (e.g., view, limited comment), with prompts indicating broader features available to authenticated users, applicable to the whole platform.

3. Encounters a public upgrade prompt, explaining the benefits and open participation model upon registration.

4. Leaves feedback or requests more information via public channels, knowing all activity is accessible to the network.

5. Decides whether to register, based on exposure to the network-wide value.

### Behavioral Traits

* Value-driven, task-focused, wary of unnecessary hurdles.

* Appreciates visible transparency in an open, public platform environment.

* Responsive to clear upgrade messaging, especially where universal access is a benefit.

### Quotes from User Research

* "The platform lets me browse and contribute in the open before asking me to register. That’s refreshing."

* "Everyone—guests and registered users—see and interact with the same content. It feels like a real community."

* "I want to see what’s possible in the open before deciding to join fully."

### Scenarios

* **Public Access and Feedback:** Val views, comments, or reviews public platform content; actions are immediately visible to the global network.

* **Upgrade Prompt:** Hits a feature barrier and is invited to join the platform to contribute more, with an explanation of network-wide participation.

* **Network Feedback:** Uses a guest interface to provide public feedback seen by all authenticated users.

* **Transparency Review:** Checks the platform’s guest privacy notice, with visibility into network-wide data use.

## Support Engineer Persona

### Persona Name

Supportive Sam

### Demographics

### Goals

* Provide rapid, empathetic support to users anywhere on the platform—no matter their configuration or context.

* Diagnose and resolve platform issues that can affect the entire global user base, knowing participation and support are not siloed.

* Follow best practice escalation and troubleshooting procedures for universal transparency and auditability.

* Ensure all support actions are visible in public logs, accessible to all authenticated users.

### Pain Points

* Navigating complex troubleshooting in an open, public network—mistakes are more easily spotted by the community.

* Managing high volume, platform-wide support requests and maintaining consistent, public communication.

* Interfacing with configuration admins while reinforcing open participation and universal impact of support actions.

* Balancing urgency with thoroughness—platform changes can have instant, broad network effects.

### User Journey

1. Receives platform-wide support requests, never org-gated—all users can submit public tickets or feedback.

2. Uses diagnostic tools and logs visible to all, ensuring transparency.

3. Provides clear, universally visible responses and documentation.

4. Escalates complex issues to platform engineering, maintaining a network-wide audit trail.

5. Closes tickets and archives resolutions that remain accessible and useful to the open community.

### Behavioral Traits

* Analytical, systematic, thrives in a public, transparent platform support role.

* Skilled communicator, emphasizes universal clarity in responses.

* Maintains high accountability, knowing all actions are globally auditable.

### Quotes from User Research

* "Anyone can see and respond to support issues—it’s key to be thorough and precise."

* "Platform-wide access means my fixes benefit the entire community, instantly."

* "Escalations and their resolutions are visible to everyone, so accuracy and transparency are critical."

### Scenarios

* **Network-Wide Ticket:** Sam fields a support issue affecting all users—publicly documents both the investigation and resolution.

* **Open Troubleshooting:** Uses platform diagnostic tools, with logs and outcomes visible to all authenticated participants.

* **Escalation:** Hands off unresolved issues, logging actions in full public view for future community learning.

* **Community Support:** Interacts with users and admins network-wide, resolving questions and building trust through transparency.

## Compliance Auditor Persona

### Persona Name

Auditor Avery

### Demographics

### Goals

* Conduct platform-wide audits, accessing all logs and evidence—public and comprehensive, not partitioned by organization.

* Verify resolution and documentation of incidents, knowing all approval workflows and findings are visible to every authenticated user.

* Ensure the platform’s open participation model meets regulatory and policy standards for every user collectively.

* Recommend improvements that benefit every participant in the platform’s open network.

### Pain Points

* Ensuring completeness and clarity in documentation for a network where every user can see audit results.

* Navigating systems where logs and evidence are entirely open, requiring high standards for accuracy and privacy.

* Coordinating reviews and interviews with stakeholders who represent the entire platform, not just specific groups.

### User Journey

1. Accesses complete, public platform logs and documentation—nothing is restricted by org or tenant.

2. Conducts review of all user and admin actions, as participation and audits are universally visible.

3. Validates that all compliance incidents, resolutions, and approvals are part of the platform’s global audit trail.

4. Aggregates findings and recommendations, submitting to the entire community for collective learning.

5. Supports follow-up and remediation processes, visible to all authenticated users for total accountability.

### Behavioral Traits

* Rigorously objective, values transparency and universal trust.

* Highly confidentiality- and privacy-oriented, balancing openness with data protection.

* Trusts but verifies—knows that auditability is greatest when everyone can see everything.

### Quotes from User Research

* "There are no hidden logs or private corners—if it happened, everyone can see it."

* "Audit results affect the whole platform. My job is to ensure we’re compliant and trustworthy at the global level."

* "Network-wide transparency keeps standards high. The community expects nothing less."

### Scenarios

* **Regulatory Platform Audit:** Avery reviews network-wide logs for GDPR or other data compliance—reports and findings are shared with the full authenticated user base.

* **Incident Postmortem:** Documents and validates a breach investigation, with records fully accessible for platform-wide review.

* **Approval Workflow Review:** Checks group and platform configuration changes, ensuring visibility and traceability for all.

* **Transparent Recommendations:** Delivers compliance recommendations openly, benefitting and addressing the entire user network.
